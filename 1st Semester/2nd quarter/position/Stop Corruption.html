What is Corruption?

Corruption is the abuse of power for personal or private gain. It can take many forms, such as bribery, embezzlement, favoritism, and fraud. It weakens democracy, slows economic development, and increases inequality, poverty, and social division.

The Impact of Corruption

Loss of Public Funds

Money meant for schools, hospitals, and infrastructure is stolen or misused.

Inequality & Injustice

Only the powerful and wealthy benefit, leaving the poor and honest behind.

Poor Public Services

Corruption leads to low-quality education, healthcare, and unsafe environments.

Erosion of Trust

People lose faith in leaders and government institutions.
